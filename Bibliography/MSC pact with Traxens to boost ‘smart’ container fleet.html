MSC pact with Traxens to boost ‘smart’ container fleet

Mediterranean Shipping Co. plans to equip 50,000 containers with tracking and sensor technology through a partnership with Traxens, in which the carrier is an investor.

The Geneva-based carrier said it will roll out the “smart” containers in the coming months, enabling users to follow the movement of containers in real time through the Internet of Things. The technology also collects and communicates real-time data on other factors that help to keep cargo secure, including temperature, humidity level, shocks and vibrations, and door opening and closing.

As cargo visibility has emerged as a priority issue for shippers and other supply chain stakeholders, ocean carriers have invested more and more in tracking technologies that enable shippers to collect key details about their in-transit cargo. Often, they rely on sensors that communicate and send information through cellphone technology, global positioning satellites, or Wi-Fi technology.

The trend has gathered pace, in part, due to the declining cost of sensors, meaning they are close to being cheap enough to discard after use rather than be returned to the carrier and reused. In addition, extending the life of batteries needed to power the sensors has made them more viable.

Smart container trend

MSC’s partnership with Traxens follows that of CMA CGM, which announced in June that it would make Traxens' technology available to all its customers. CMA CGM was Traxens’ first investor.

Also in June, Maersk Line said it had invested in a US-based startup called SensorTransport, which makes inexpensive, disposable tracking devices meant to be affixed directly to goods to monitor motion, humidity, and temperature, as well as other factors. In a separate project, the carrier in 2015 began putting sensors into its entire 270,000 fleet of refrigerated boxes, to monitor the temperature of the boxes and to improve efficiency in the scheduling of the box maintenance.

Traxens, a French startup that makes container sensors, has developed an associated data platform to give shippers information about the container’s location, outside temperature, impacts, movement, and vibration. Data collected by the permanently installed sensors, called TRAXENS-BOX S+, are relayed to a radio network that the company developed to cope with the obstacles of the physical shipping environment. For instance, the radio network is designed to reach boxes deep in the holds of the world’s largest container ships. The sensors can also detect when a container door has been opened and will soon be able to detect the temperature and humidity levels inside the container.

In a statement, MSC said it expects the smart containers to bring substantial gains in efficiency, service, security and safety along the entire supply chain. The technology “will also help manage delays and other problems along the supply chain where historically chasing information has been time-consuming and expensive,” the release said.

“MSC believes that the real-time tracking of containers is the future of the shipping industry,” said Diego Aponte, president and CEO of MSC Group. “While shipping lines should of course compete on service, we will achieve better results for our customers by working in a more harmonised way on technology and innovation. Smart containers are a perfect example of where we can cooperate according to industry standards to make our services truly comprehensive.”
